# Prompt: How should we actually use injury/suspension/red-card data to improve an FPL bot?

## Context

I'm building **PLFantasyBot**, a bot that picks a Fantasy Premier League (FPL) squad and manages it gameweek-by-gameweek (transfers, captaincy, chips), backtested against real historical seasons. It's a Python project: a gradient-boosted regression model predicts each player's points for a gameweek, and an ILP optimizer picks the best legal squad/lineup given those predictions, subject to FPL's real rules (budget, formation, transfer limits, chips).

I've spent a long session trying to use **player injury/suspension/availability data** to improve this bot. I tried eight different approaches. All eight failed or were neutral. I want a genuinely fresh, rigorous look at *why*, and at what might actually work that I haven't thought of — not a rehash of the same ideas with different thresholds.

## Architecture (so you understand exactly how decisions are made)

**Data**: Two sources.
1. [vaastav/Fantasy-Premier-League](https://github.com/vaastav/Fantasy-Premier-League) — historical per-gameweek results for every PL season back to 2016-17: goals, assists, minutes, bonus, BPS, ICT index, price, clean sheets, cards, etc. This is *retrospective* — it records what happened in a match, nothing about what was known beforehand.
2. [Randdalf/fplcache](https://github.com/Randdalf/fplcache) — a community archive that has snapshotted FPL's live `bootstrap-static` API endpoint 4x/day since April 2021. Each snapshot includes every player's real-time `status` (`a`=available, `d`=doubtful, `i`=injured, `s`=suspended, `u`=unavailable/not registered), `chance_of_playing_next_round` (0/25/50/75/100/null), and free-text `news`. This is genuinely *point-in-time* — I fetch the nearest snapshot before each historical gameweek's deadline, so it reflects exactly what a real manager would have known before selecting their team that week, with no lookahead into results.

**Prediction model** (`train_model.py`): `sklearn.GradientBoostingRegressor` (200 trees, depth 3, lr 0.05, fixed `random_state=42`), trained on 2020-21→2024-25 (~130k player-gameweek rows), predicting `total_points` for a gameweek. Current features: rolling 3-and-5-gameweek averages (shifted so a gameweek's features never include its own outcome) of total_points, minutes, goals, assists, BPS, ICT index, influence, creativity, threat, clean_sheets; plus price (`value`), home/away, and opponent fixture difficulty (FPL's 1-5 FDR). Current backtest accuracy on the 2025-26 held-out season: MAE 0.991, correlation 0.572 (vs. baselines of MAE 1.583/correlation 0.00 for "predict the mean" and MAE 1.059/correlation 0.498 for "predict last-5-gameweek average").

**Optimizer** (`optimizer.py`): `scipy.optimize.milp` (HiGHS solver). `select_squad(pool, budget, current_ids=None, max_changes=None)` picks 15 players maximizing summed `predicted_points`, subject to: exactly 2 GKP/5 DEF/5 MID/3 FWD, budget in tenths of £m, max 3 players per club, and (if `current_ids`/`max_changes` given) at most `max_changes` players may differ from the current squad. `pick_starting_xi(squad)` brute-forces all 7 valid FPL formations (3-4-3 through 5-4-1) to pick the best-scoring legal XI + bench. `pick_captains(xi)` returns the top-2 by `predicted_points` as captain/vice.

**Season simulator** (`simulate_season.py`) — the core loop, one iteration per gameweek:
1. Build `predicted_points` for every player this gameweek (model inference on frozen rolling-form features).
2. Build a **horizon score** for squad-construction decisions: sum of *decayed* predicted points across every remaining gameweek in the season (`LOOKAHEAD_DECAY = 0.85` per week ahead — a confidence discount, since a prediction 20 weeks out is much less trustworthy than next week's). Future weeks' fixture/difficulty/home-away are real (the schedule is public in advance), but rolling-form features are frozen at current values (we don't know future form).
3. **GW1**: `select_squad` picks a fresh 15 from the whole player pool, budget £100.0m.
4. **Wildcard weeks (fixed at GW8 and GW20)**: full unconstrained re-optimization within current squad value.
5. **Normal weeks**: search `k = 0..min(free_transfers+2, 5)` transfers; for each `k`, `select_squad(..., current_ids=owned, max_changes=k)` finds the best squad reachable with exactly `k` changes; pick whichever `k` maximizes `horizon_value - 4×max(0, k - free_transfers)`. Free transfers start at 1/week, roll over up to a cap of 5.
6. **Starting XI + captain**: chosen from the resulting 15 using *this week's* `predicted_points` only (not the horizon score — you always want your best lineup this week regardless of the run of form ahead).
7. **Triple Captain** (once per half-season, GW1-19 and GW20-38): fires on the first gameweek where the best captain-eligible forward has an easy fixture (FDR ≤ 2) and clears a form threshold; forced on the last gameweek of the half if never naturally triggered, so it isn't wasted. Bench Boost is fixed at GW9/GW21 (the gameweek after each Wildcard).
8. **Scoring uses the real historical outcome**, not the prediction: `total_points` and `minutes` for whichever players were actually selected.
9. **Auto-substitution** (this is the critical mechanism relevant to the injury-data question): after the real gameweek result is known, any starter whose real `minutes == 0` is automatically swapped for the highest-scoring bench player whose real `minutes > 0`, provided the resulting lineup still satisfies formation minimums (≥1 GKP, ≥3 DEF, ≥2 MID, ≥1 FWD). This exactly mirrors FPL's real auto-substitution rule.
10. **Vice-captain fallback**: if the captain's real minutes are 0, the vice-captain's score is used for the captaincy multiplier instead.

**Validation discipline**: every change is tested with `multi_season_backtest.py` across **three independent seasons** (2023-24, 2024-25, 2025-26), each trained only on seasons strictly before it (no leakage), and compared against that season's real average-manager score (the actual sum of `average_entry_score` across all 38 gameweeks — pulled from Wayback Machine snapshots of `bootstrap-static` for past seasons, since FPL's live API only serves the current season). A change is only kept if it's better-or-equal across most of the three seasons — single-season results are treated as noise, not evidence, because early findings in this project showed decay-parameter sweeps bouncing around non-monotonically across seasons.

## What I've tried with injury/suspension data (all reverted, none currently in the codebase)

**Attempt 4 — availability as a model feature + hard `<50%` XI/captaincy exclusion, combined.** Merged real `chance_of_playing_next_round` into the training data as a genuine model feature (fillna 100 = assume fit when no news exists), *and* separately zeroed `predicted_points` for anyone under 50% chance before picking the starting XI/captain. Single-gameweek MAE improved substantially: 0.991 → 0.942, the biggest gain of any feature tried, and I explicitly verified (not assumed) that the improvement was real for squad-relevant players too (top-150-predicted/gameweek), not just free MAE from fringe players nobody would pick anyway (+0.067 MAE gain for relevant players, +0.045 for fringe). But the full-season result: 2023-24 → 1976 (-80, below average manager), 2024-25 → 2230 (+81), 2025-26 → 2024 (-34). Net negative, mixed direction. Reverted.

**Attempt 5 — isolated the XI/captaincy filter alone** (same `<50%` cutoff, but *not* fed to the model this time — model predictions completely unchanged, MAE unaffected). 2023-24 → 2041 (-15), 2024-25 → 2073 (-76), 2025-26 → 2058 (**exactly identical to baseline**). I verified directly that zero flagged low-availability players were ever actually started — the filter worked mechanically — yet the season score for 2025-26 didn't move at all. This revealed why: **auto-substitution and vice-captain fallback already retroactively correct for any blanked player, using the real, certain outcome.** A pre-emptive filter based on a probability can only ever match that (when it guesses right) or lose to it (when a "doubtful" player actually plays and scores, but has already been benched in advance). Reverted.

**Attempt 6 — same mechanism, stricter threshold** (`<25%` chance, i.e. only exclude if >75% likely out). 2023-24 → 2044 (-12), 2024-25 → 2079 (-70), 2025-26 → 2058 (identical). Less damage than the 50% threshold, exactly as predicted by the theory above, but still net negative. Reverted.

**Attempt 7 — the limiting case: a near-certain signal instead of a probabilistic one.** Instead of injury doubt, used red cards: verified directly against real data (not assumed) that 79-96% of red-carded players get 0 minutes the following gameweek across three separate seasons (53/55 in 2023-24 alone) — and this needed *no* external data at all, since `red_cards` was already in the historical dataset. Applied the same way: never start/captain someone sent off last gameweek. Result: **exactly identical to baseline in all three seasons** (2056/2149/2058, byte-for-byte). This is the cleanest confirmation of the theory: because red cards are near-certain, the pre-emptive filter and the reactive auto-sub/vice-captain-fallback reach the same conclusion almost every time, so there's no downside (nothing gets wrongly guessed) but also no upside (auto-subs already caught it for free). Reverted — not because it hurt, but because it's provably dead code.

**Attempt 8 — moved to the untried application: transfer/wildcard *value*, not XI/captaincy.** Reasoning: attempts 4-7 all failed because XI selection has a free, perfect, after-the-fact correction (auto-subs) that any pre-emptive signal is competing against and can't beat. But *ownership* decisions (who to transfer in/out, who to wildcard onto the squad) have no equivalent free correction — a bad buy costs a real transfer or a `-4` hit to undo. So: scaled each player's *current-gameweek* contribution to their horizon score by their real chance-of-playing (a **soft, proportional expected-value discount** — a 60%-fit player contributes 60% of their predicted points — not a hard cutoff), applied only to the horizon score used for `select_squad` calls (initial squad, wildcard, transfer search), and left completely untouched: the model, the weekly `predicted_points` used for XI/captaincy, and every future week in the horizon sum beyond the current one (since a real manager doesn't know next month's fitness news any more than the bot does). Result: 2023-24 → 2050 (-6), 2024-25 → 2058 (-91), 2025-26 → 2025 (-33). **Worse in all three seasons** — the most consistently negative result of any attempt, even though no single season's drop was the largest seen. Reverted.

My own (explicitly labeled as unverified, time-boxed) hypothesis for attempt 8: discounting only the current week while leaving the next several weeks undiscounted creates an inconsistency — a transient, one-week knock (80% today, presumably 100% next week) dents the horizon score just enough to occasionally tip a marginal hold-vs-transfer decision, without reflecting that the player will likely be fine again soon. Since the horizon is recomputed fresh every gameweek, this could cause selling low and wanting to buy back a week later, burning a real transfer or hit on noise that would have resolved itself for free. I did not directly measure this (e.g., count of extra transfers/hits attributable to availability swings) before reverting due to time constraints — so treat it as a lead, not a finding.
