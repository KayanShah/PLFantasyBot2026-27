# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

This is a small hobby project, but everyone interacting with it — through issues, pull requests, discussions, or any other contribution — should feel welcome to do so, regardless of experience level, background, or how they found the repo.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and constructive, especially when disagreeing about an approach or reviewing someone's code.
- Give feedback the way it's given throughout this project's own history in [`plan.md`](plan.md): honestly, with evidence, and without making it personal. "This regressed the score, here's why" is welcome. "This is a bad idea" without a reason isn't useful to anyone.
- Assume good faith. Most mistakes here are genuinely mistakes, not carelessness.
- Respect that this is maintained by one person in their own time — response times may be slow.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks directed at anyone participating in the project.
- Discriminatory language or behavior of any kind.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.
- Any other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or community setting.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces — issues, pull requests, commit messages, code review comments, and any other official channel — as well as anyone representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior can be reported by emailing **[hi@kayanshah.com](mailto:hi@kayanshah.com)**. Reports will be reviewed and handled as appropriate, which may include a warning, temporary restriction, or permanent ban from participating in the project.

Maintainers are expected to apply this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently.
